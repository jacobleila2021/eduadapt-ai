1. What real-world problem are you solving?

Teachers spend 2–5 hours adapting a single lesson for students with different learning needs, including dyslexia, ADHD, English language learners, struggling readers, 
and advanced learners. Due to time constraints, many lessons are not fully differentiated, limiting accessibility and inclusion. 
This increases teacher workload, contributes to burnout, and prevents students from receiving learning experiences tailored to their needs. 
EduAdapt AI solves this by automating lesson differentiation, reducing preparation time from hours to minutes while maintaining instructional quality.

2. Who is the problem for?

EduAdapt AI is designed for teachers, special educators, learning support teams, and schools serving students in Grades 3–11. 
It primarily supports educators responsible for inclusive classrooms where learners have diverse academic, cognitive, language, and learning needs. 
The solution also indirectly benefits students by providing more accessible and personalized learning materials, ensuring that every learner can engage with grade-level content 
in a way that matches their learning profile.

3. How does your solution use AI?

EduAdapt AI uses generative AI and natural language understanding to analyze unstructured lesson content uploaded by teachers. 
The AI identifies learning objectives, concepts, vocabulary, and complexity levels, then automatically generates differentiated versions for dyslexia, ADHD, English language learners, 
advanced learners, and struggling readers. It also creates classroom activities, teacher differentiation notes, and UDL-aligned recommendations. 
AI performs instructional adaptation tasks that normally require professional educational judgment, transforming a process that takes hours into one completed in minutes.
